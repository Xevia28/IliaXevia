Project Overview

Elevate is a cross-platform learning companion built to support teenagers in mastering college-level subjects with confidence and clarity. Designed with a student-first approach, Elevate goes beyond traditional study tools by fostering deep understanding through interactive lessons, collaborative features, and personalized learning paths. Whether it's preparing for exams, working through complex topics, or engaging in peer discussions, Elevate empowers students to take control of their learning journey. With a strong emphasis on comprehension, engagement, and academic growth, it transforms studying into a more meaningful and effective experience.

Our learning platform, Elevate, is designed to help students navigate college-level subjects through structured learning paths, interactive tools, and collaborative study features. It offers instant support, peer-to-peer discussions, and personalized progress tracking‚Äîspecifically tailored to meet the needs of teenage learners. By providing clear guidance and fostering an engaging learning environment, Elevate reduces confusion around complex topics, enhances motivation, and makes advanced academic content more approachable. With its focus on accessibility, support, and collaboration, Elevate empowers students to build lasting understanding and confidence in their studies.

The Problem

Teenagers studying college-level subjects often struggle with understanding complex concepts clearly and staying motivated. Many rely on fragmented resources, study alone, or face difficulties collaborating effectively with peers. Existing learning tools tend to focus on rote memorization rather than true comprehension, leaving students feeling overwhelmed, disconnected, and lacking confidence in their academic progress.

The Goal

To design and develop Elevate, a cross-platform learning companion that helps teenagers excel in challenging college subjects. Elevate aims to foster clarity through simplified explanations, encourage collaboration via shared study spaces, and build confidence by providing personalized support. The platform's objective is to transform study time from passive memorization into active, deep understanding, empowering students to master content and achieve academic success.

User Research: Pain Points

Lack of Conceptual Clarity

Students reported difficulty in grasping complex college-level topics through traditional resources, which often presented information in a dense or overly technical manner.

Feeling Isolated During Study

Many teenagers found studying alone demotivating and wished for easy ways to collaborate and discuss problems with peers in real time.

Overwhelm from Fragmented Resources

Users struggled to organize notes, references, and study materials scattered across multiple apps, leading to inefficient study sessions.

Low Confidence and Anxiety

Students expressed a lack of confidence in their understanding and exam readiness, often feeling anxious about their performance without adequate support.

Design

Paper Wireframe

I started with rough sketches to brainstorm layout ideas and map out the core user journey. The focus was on clarity, ensuring that students could easily navigate the platform without feeling lost or overwhelmed. Key sections like the Study Dashboard, Topic Explorer, and Group Study Rooms were separated to keep the flow intuitive and reduce cognitive load during onboarding.

Digital Wireframe

Once the foundational layout was validated, I moved into Figma to develop digital wireframes. I created a clean and consistent design system with a focus on readability and accessibility. The main dashboard acts as a central command center, allowing students to jump into active topics, track their progress, and join or start collaborative study sessions.

Low-Fidelity Prototypes

Using Figma's prototyping features, I linked screens to simulate essential user interactions. The low-fidelity prototype followed a typical user journey, from selecting a subject and diving into a topic, to joining a group study room and tracking individual mastery. Features like concept checklists, real-time chat, and synced notes were highlighted to support collaborative and confident learning.

The aim was to evaluate how students interact with Elevate‚Äôs learning modules, study tools, and collaborative features, and to identify friction points in comprehension, engagement, and motivation.

üìà Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

  • Time on Task: Average time to complete a topic or module. Time spent on tools like quizzes, flashcards, and video lessons.
  • User Error Rates: Frequency of skipped content, drop-offs, or abandonment due to unclear navigation or frustration.
  • Conversion Rates: Percentage of new users completing a full learning path and joining study groups or seeking AI/peer help.
  • User Satisfaction: Post-module survey scores on clarity, pacing, and helpfulness. Qualitative feedback on explanation style and visual aids.
  • Engagement: Average number of topics completed per student weekly. Revisit rate for revision tools like flashcards or tests.

üß† Synthesized Insights

  1. Need for Better Entry Points for Quick Review
    Students struggled to find tools for fast revision before tests.
    Recommendation: Add a "Quick Review" mode with summaries and flashcard previews.
  2. Overwhelming Lesson Length
    Long or dense lessons without time estimates discouraged users.
    Recommendation: Break up lessons and display estimated completion times.
  3. AI Tutor Guidance Unclear
    Students were unsure how to use AI support tools.
    Recommendation: Provide sample prompts, walkthroughs, and Q&A examples.
  4. Low Peer Interaction
    Inactive groups diminished the social learning experience.
    Recommendation: Match users by activity/subject and promote trending discussions.
  5. Incomplete Content Is Frustrating
    Missing modules led to confusion and trust issues.
    Recommendation: Hide unreleased content or label it with expected release info. Let users subscribe to updates.

Refining The Design

After analyzing the usability study findings, I refined Elevate‚Äôs design to directly address core usability issues and improve the learning experience. To support students looking for fast revision, I introduced a ‚ÄúQuick Review‚Äù mode with flashcard previews and summary sheets, helping users prepare more efficiently before assessments. For better pacing, I added time estimates to each lesson and broke dense modules into smaller, digestible parts. The AI tutor was enhanced with sample prompts and an onboarding walkthrough, guiding students on how to engage with the tool effectively. To improve peer collaboration, I implemented auto-matching based on subject interest and recent activity, ensuring students join active groups and discussions. I also updated the interface to clearly label or temporarily hide unfinished content with "Coming Soon" indicators, reducing confusion and maintaining user trust. These refinements collectively enhanced clarity, motivation, and engagement‚Äîlaying a strong foundation for more personalized learning and future feature iterations.
