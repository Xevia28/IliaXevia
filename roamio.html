Project Overview

Roamio is a student-focused trip booking platform crafted to simplify group travel planning by making it social, collaborative, and hassle-free. It combines tools for group coordination, destination exploration, and seamless trip management into a single, user-friendly platform. Whether organizing a weekend getaway or a longer adventure, Roamio helps students collaborate effortlessly, discover exciting locations, and keep all travel details in one place—reducing stress and enhancing the fun of planning trips together.

Our student trip planning app, Roamio, empowers users to explore exciting destinations, create detailed itineraries, and book trips with ease—all while collaborating in real time with their group. Featuring tools for cost splitting and access to curated, student-friendly options, Roamio streamlines the entire planning process. By minimizing stress and enhancing coordination, it makes group travel more accessible, affordable, and enjoyable for students looking to make the most of their adventures together.

The Problem

Planning group trips as a student is often chaotic and frustrating. Most students rely on a mix of messaging apps, spreadsheets, and verbal coordination, leading to disorganized plans, last-minute confusion, and unfair cost distribution. There's no single platform that caters specifically to student needs like real-time collaboration, safe destination discovery, and easy cost-sharing.

The Goal

To design and build Roamio, a student-centric trip planning and booking platform that simplifies group getaways. The platform enables users to collaboratively plan trips, discover student-friendly destinations, split expenses fairly, and book travel and stays, all within one intuitive app. Our objective was to create a seamless and social travel-planning experience tailored to how students actually organize group outings.

User Research: Pain Points

Coordination Takes Too Much Time

Students reported that planning group trips was time-consuming due to scattered communication across multiple platforms like WhatsApp and Instagram. Organizing transport, accommodation, and activities often led to delays and miscommunication.

Delays and Planning Drop-Offs

Without a centralized system to manage updates or decisions, students often lost momentum during the planning process. A lack of real-time collaboration meant some group members were left out of decisions or didn't stay informed, leading to incomplete plans.

Limited Visibility into Group Input

Students were unsure if changes made to itineraries or trip details were visible to others. This created confusion around what had been finalized and what was still in progress, undermining trust in the group planning process.

Blank States Confused Users

Empty sections—like destination reviews—were often interpreted as broken features. When no content was available, the UI provided no context, making the app feel incomplete or buggy to new users.

User Journey

Design

Paper Wireframe

I began with quick sketches to explore layout options and user flow. The primary goal was to keep the interface clean and intuitive, especially for first-time users. To avoid overwhelming the user with information, I separated the Welcome screen from the Destination Explorer and Itinerary Builder. This approach ensures a smooth and gradual onboarding experience.

Digital Wireframe

After validating the structure on paper, I transitioned to Figma to design digital wireframes. I developed a consistent design system that emphasized usability and clarity. The Home Dashboard serves as the central hub, offering students immediate access to trip plans, destination suggestions, and recent activity.

Low-Fidelity Prototypes

Using Figma's prototyping tools, I connected the wireframes to simulate core interactions. The low-fidelity prototype maps the primary user journey—from discovering a destination and building an itinerary, to inviting friends and finalizing bookings. Interactive elements like voting on trip stops, viewing shared expenses, and switching between confirmed vs. pending plans were emphasized to reflect real group dynamics.

Usability Study

To evaluate Roamio's effectiveness as a group trip planning tool, I conducted a moderated usability study with five student participants aged 22-32. The goal was to assess how users interact with the platform's core features, such as itinerary building, cost-splitting, and destination discovery, and identify pain points that impact the group planning experience.

📌 Research Objectives

  • How long does it take for a group of 4–6 students to plan and confirm a trip using Roamio?
  • What challenges do users face when coordinating trip details with friends?
  • Are the itinerary builder and destination filters intuitive and helpful?
  • Does the cost-splitting feature improve group collaboration and reduce planning fatigue?
  • Do curated, student-reviewed destinations inspire user confidence and increase booking rates?

📈 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

  • Time on Task: Time to complete a trip plan and use itinerary/cost-split tools.
  • User Error Rates: Drop-offs or abandoned trips due to confusion or cost clarity.
  • Conversion Rates: Trips resulting in confirmed bookings and invited collaborators.
  • User Satisfaction: Post-survey scores on ease of use and planning clarity.
  • Engagement: Average number of trips created per user per semester.

🧍 Participant Feedback & Insights

Participant 1 (Male, 22)
“I was looking for budget-friendly destinations but didn't see any way to filter by cost.”
➡ Users want trip discovery tools that align with personal constraints like cost and activity type.

Participant 2 (Female, 22)
“I tried building an itinerary, but I couldn't tell if my friends would see the updates.”
➡ Real-time collaboration features need to be more transparent and clearly communicated.

Participant 3 (Male, 23)
“Splitting costs was confusing. I wasn't sure how much I personally owed.”
➡ Users need clearer breakdowns of group expenses to ensure fairness and reduce financial stress.

Participant 4 (Female, 32)
“Some trip pages didn't show any reviews—I thought they were broken.”
➡ Empty states must be handled with better UI cues to avoid perceived app failure.

Participant 5 (Male, 24)
“Inviting friends took too many steps. It should be simpler—just let me send a link.”
➡ Users value quick, frictionless group-sharing features to encourage participation.

🧠 Synthesized Insights

  1. Limited Filtering Options
    Users want more control when browsing destinations.
    Recommendation: Add filters for cost and activity type (e.g., hikes, nature spots, food trips).
  2. Lack of Group Planning Visibility
    Users felt unsure about what itinerary updates were visible to others.
    Recommendation: Introduce visual indicators for shared and pending items in itineraries.
  3. Unclear Cost-Splitting Experience
    Ambiguous expense tracking discouraged user confidence.
    Recommendation: Display per-person totals, who paid, and outstanding balances clearly.
  4. Confusing Empty States
    Blank sections created uncertainty and reduced trust.
    Recommendation: Use placeholder content such as "No reviews yet, be the first to share your thoughts!"

Refining The Design

After conducting the usability study, I refined Roamio's design to address key user pain points and enhance the overall experience. I introduced budget and activity-based filters to make destination discovery more relevant and efficient, reducing decision fatigue. To improve group coordination, I added visual indicators for itinerary updates—such as pending and confirmed status—to ensure all members were aligned. The cost-splitting feature was also updated with per-person summaries and clear payment breakdowns, making shared expenses more transparent and fair. Additionally, I streamlined the group invitation process by enabling one-click sharing via a link, which significantly simplified collaboration. To avoid confusion from blank pages, I implemented placeholder messages like "No reviews yet" to maintain user trust and clarity. These refinements helped create a smoother, more intuitive planning flow, and set the foundation for future iterations including visual styling and onboarding enhancements.
